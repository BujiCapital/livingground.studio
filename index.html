Case Study

The Transformation of Polo Ranch

300 Village Lane — from unfinished lot to living ecosystem.

Before

A blank canvas waiting to become a living ecosystem

The property began as an unfinished landscape — bare soil, no shade, no habitat. Beautiful home, incomplete experience. The homeowners wanted an outdoor space that matched the intention of their interior: designed, living, purposeful.

Design

Reading the land, mapping the vision

We mapped sun patterns, wind corridors, soil composition, and drainage. Then we designed zones: an outdoor kitchen for gathering, a food forest for the family, native habitat corridors for wildlife, and a shaded terrace for morning coffee.

Build

Soil, stone, water, and 200 plants

Our crews restored the soil biology, laid natural stone pathways, installed drip irrigation, planted fruit trees, native perennials, edible ground covers, and built the outdoor kitchen. Eight weeks from breaking ground to first harvest.

After

This is what happens when a backyard comes alive

Eighteen months later: citrus trees bearing fruit, hummingbirds nesting in the native sage, dinners under the pergola three nights a week, and a landscape that requires less water than the lawn it replaced. The homeowners' only regret: not starting sooner.
