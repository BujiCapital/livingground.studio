Featured Project
300 Village Lane, Polo Ranch, Scotts Valley

From blank canvas
to living ecosystem.

Every project begins with the same question: what does this land want to become? Here's how we answered it for one family at Polo Ranch — a newly constructed home in the redwood foothills of Scotts Valley with a yard of nothing but compacted dirt.

Phase 01 -- Before

The starting point:
beautiful home, bare earth.

Like many new-build luxury homes, this property was delivered with every interior detail perfected -- and an exterior of compacted fill dirt. No drainage plan. No outdoor living. No connection between the home and the stunning redwood hills surrounding it.

Walk through the property as it was delivered — 360° interactive tour

Phase 02 -- Design

Reading the land,
mapping the vision.

We studied the property's microclimates, soil composition, drainage patterns, and sun exposure. We listened to the family's rhythms -- how they cook, gather, play. The design emerged from both: a landscape that serves the people and the place.

Phase 03 -- Build

Shaping the ground,
placing every stone.

Hardscape installation, grading and drainage infrastructure, irrigation systems, soil amendment, and strategic planting. Every element placed with purpose -- from the decomposed granite pathways to the native plantings along the fence line. Built to age beautifully.

Phase 04 -- After

A home that finally
meets its land.

Where bare dirt once baked in the sun, a living ecosystem now thrives. Fruit trees bearing nectarines and passionfruit. Native flowers attracting pollinators. Mulched pathways under vine-covered arbors. A landscape that produces food, manages water, and looks more beautiful every season.

Polo Ranch — passionfruit on the arbor, nectarines ripening, native flowers blooming. Matterport after tour coming soon.

This property is now listed for sale — a testament to how the right landscape transforms not just a home, but its value.
